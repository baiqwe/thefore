## Why Heavy Armor Rules the Economy

I analyzed every craftable item in the game. Heavy Armor wins for one simple reason: **Material Cost vs. Selling Price**.

NPC merchants pay based on two factors:
1. Equipment rarity
2. Total materials consumed

Since Heavy Armor uses more ingots than weapons, its base value is significantly higher.

## Step-by-Step Profit Strategy

Here is my exact workflow that generates 90,000+ gold per hour.

### Step 1: Efficient Material Collection

Don't waste time on rare ores. Volume is king.

- **Target:** Common Ore & Copper Ore
- **Location:** [Newbie Mine](/blog/my-blacksmith-journey-begins)
- **Goal:** 100+ ores per 30-minute session

> **Pro Tip:** Use a [Fae or Elf race](/blog/quest-for-dragonborn-bloodline) for faster gathering speeds if you have them.

### Step 2: Perfect Quality Smelting

Quality dramatically affects selling price. A "Perfect" ingot is worth far more than an "Average" one.

| Smelting Result | Ingot Quality | Price Multiplier |
|-----------------|---------------|------------------|
| Slag | Worthless | 0x (Loss) |
| Dross | Poor | 0.5x |
| Average | Standard | 1.0x |
| Perfect | High | **1.8-2.2x** |

**My Rule:** Never rush smelting. Use [temperature mastery techniques](/blog/furnace-temperature-mastery) to hit the Golden Zone every time.

| Merchant Location | Markup | Travel Time | Verdict |

I always travel to **High Forge City**. On a full inventory of 10 armor pieces, that 8-minute trip earns me an extra 50,000 gold.

## Advanced Techniques

Once you master the basics, use these tips to maximize profits.

### 1. Bulk Crafting Flow

Don't craft one piece at a time.

1. Gather materials for 10 full sets
2. Smelt all ingots in one go
3. Forge all armor pieces back-to-back

This "batch processing" puts you in a flow state, improving your [perfect strike rate](/blog/my-blacksmith-journey-begins).

### 2. Market Timing

Player demand affects prices.

- **Weekdays:** Prices are stable. Craft and stockpile.
- **Weekends:** Prices spike due to high player count. Sell everything.

### 3. Material Speculation

When updates are announced, material prices go crazy.

> **Example:** When the Ice Dungeon was announced, I bought 500 Fire Ores cheap. Two weeks later, I sold them for 300% profit to players needing fire gear.

Keep an eye on [trading trends](/blog/advanced-trading-secrets).

## Common Profit Killers

Avoid these mistakes that eat into your margins.

### Mistake 1: Crafting for Looks
I used to make Epic helmets because they looked cool. They sold for less than simple chest plates. **Craft for stats and value, not aesthetics.**

### Mistake 2: Ignoring Tool Durability
High-tier pickaxes are expensive to repair. Don't use your [Legendary Arcane Pickaxe](/blog/legendary-arcane-pickaxe-guide) to mine basic copper. Use a cheaper tool for bulk farming.

### Mistake 3: Hoarding
Inventory space is money. Don't hold onto "maybe useful" items. If you don't need it for a specific build, sell it or [deconstruct it](/blog/inventory-management-philosophy).
