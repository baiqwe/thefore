# The Forge (Roblox) Anvil Guide: Mastering the Perfect Strike

**Is your hammer a blunt instrument or a precision tool?**

Friends, if you're like me, you probably spent your early days in **The Forge on Roblox** obsessing over mining speed. But veteran players taught me a crucial truth: Anvil Mastery is the real bottleneck.

Achieving the "Perfect Strike" is the only way to create truly [legendary gear](/blog/legendary-arcane-pickaxe-guide). My hammer used to be a brute force tool; now, it's a precision instrument.

## The Chaos of the Click

My initial approach to the forging mini-game was pure chaos. When that shrinking circle appeared, I'd frantically click, hoping to land a hit.

I managed to craft basic swords, sure. But their "Quality Score" was always mediocre, even with [high-tier fuel](/blog/furnace-fuel-economics).

## The Predictive Click

This was my biggest breakthrough.

Instead of waiting to see the alignment, I learned to click **slightly before** they align. This accounts for the game's slight input lag.

> **Pro Tip:** If you play on a server with high ping, you must click even earlier. Test your latency before crafting [Heavy Armor for profit](/blog/heavy-armor-crafting-for-profit).

My clicks became predictive, not reactive. My Perfect Strike rate went from 20% to 85% overnight.

## The "Hammer Stance" Trick

This is an advanced technique I found on the forums.

Depending on the angle you approach the Anvil, the swing animation feels subtly different.

**My Sweet Spot:**
- Stand at the **corner** of the Anvil.
- Camera angle: Top-down (bird's eye view).

The visual cue feels most natural here. I stand there religiously for every high-stakes forging attempt, especially when working with [Arcane Ore](/blog/legendary-arcane-pickaxe-guide).
